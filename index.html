Why we need Renewable Energy?

Renewable energy, harnessing sunlight, wind, and water, plays a critical role in addressing global environmental challenges. It helps mitigate global warming caused by greenhouse gas emissions and reduces dependency on finite resources. In 2021, renewable energy accounted for 28.4% of global electricity generation, according to the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA). However, deployment varies due to factors such as policy support, technology costs, and infrastructure. This dashboard explores the state of renewable energy and sustainability around the world.

Global Electricity Accessibility in 2020

In 2020, most countries globally achieved electricity accessibility of over 80% for their populations. However, several countries, primarily in Africa, had access rates below 50% due to insufficient infrastructure and technology.

Future Energy Mix

As more countries invest in renewables, the global energy mix is expected to shift significantly. Nuclear power will remain a key player in Europe and parts of Asia, while renewable sources are expected to grow exponentially.

Oceania is the only continent with no nuclear power use. In Europe, nuclear power dominates with 132 reactors generating 25.6% of its electricity (totaling 876,244 GWh in 2021). South Africa is the only African nation with nuclear power, contributing around 5% of its electricity via two reactors.

Energy Intensity Trends Across Top 20 GDP Countries

Energy intensity measures energy use per unit of economic output. Countries like Iceland have high energy intensity, meaning energy consumption strongly supports their GDP. By contrast, Switzerland and Ireland have low energy intensity, indicating more efficient energy use.
